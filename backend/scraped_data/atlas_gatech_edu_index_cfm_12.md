## Program Overview

Bangalore, India is hub for aerospace industry and government labs such as Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL), National Aeronautics Laboratory (NAL), Aeronautical development Establishment (ADE), Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), Gas Turbine Research Establishment (GTRE), etc. In addition, several of the US and European aerospace industry have overseas research and development centers in Bangalore, India, such as Boeing, Bell Textron, Airbus, GE Global Research center, etc. Faculty at Indian Institute of Science conduct cutting-edge research in aerospace sciences with expansive research facilities, such as Laboratory for hypersonic and shock wave research, Turbulent Shear Flow Physics and Engineering Lab, Combustion, Gasification and Propulsion Laboratory (CGPL), Integrative Multi-scale Engineering Materials and Systems (iMEMS) lab, Advanced Materials and Processing Lab (AMPL), etc. This program combinesÂ course work with organized local aerospace industry tours, and lectures and lab torus organized by the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Aerospace Department faculty.
